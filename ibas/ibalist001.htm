Bicuari National Park covers a large area (7900 km2) of miombo and teak woodlands on Kalahari sands in Huila province in the south of Angola (15 08S, 14 56E). The avifauna is relatively poorly known, with a list of 143 species reported by Dean (2001) based on survey work prior to 1974. Data came from a single day visit by Dean in Septermber 1973 during which 74 species were recorded and a list of collected specimens from Mulondo on the south-east border of the park (source/s unknown).

The only subsequent visit was by Mills in January 2009, for just two days. During this time 88 species were recorded, of which about half (43) were new to the park list, bringing the total to 186 species (see below). The rate at which new species were added during a short visit suggests that the list is substantially larger.

Of these 186 species listed below, two should be treated as unconfirmed (see comments).


References

Dean WRJ. 2001. Angola. Pages 71–91 in Fishpool LDC and Evans MI (eds) Important bird areas in Africa and associated islands: priority sites for conservation. Newbury and Cambridge, UK: Pisces Publications and BirdLife International.
