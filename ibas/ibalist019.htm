Mount Moco is the highest mountain in Angola and protects the second largest area of Afromontane forest in the country. Most importantly it holds a significant population of the Endangered Swierstra's Francolin.

The avifauna of this IBA is now well known, with several collecting trips prior to the mid-1970s and more than ten visits by Michael Mills between 2005 and the present. All data prior to 2010 is summarised by Mills et al. (2011) and forms the basis for this list, with additional species since then reported with the observer and date.


References

MILLS MSL, OLMOS F, MELO M and DEAN WRJ. 2011. Mount Moco: its importance to the conservation of Swierstra’s Francolin Pternistis swierstrai and the Afromontane avifauna of Angola. Bird Conservation International 21:119–133.
