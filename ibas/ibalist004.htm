Camabatela is situated in Cuanza Norte province of north-western Angola (08 11S, 15 23E) on the edge of the northern scarp forests, although the limits of the IBA are poorly defined. The actual IBA lies at least 20 km to the west of the town where the scarp forests hold a significant population of the Endangered Braun's Bushshrike.

The area was relatively well collected by Braun (as summarised in Dean 2001) and the list, based on older data, consisted of 215 species.

Since then MSL Mills (unpublished data) has made a couple of brief visits to the area (2009-2013), recording 66 species of which only 3 were new. The total list for the area is now 218.


References

Dean WRJ. 2001. Angola. Pages 71–91 in Fishpool LDC and Evans MI (eds) Important bird areas in Africa and associated islands: priority sites for conservation. Newbury and Cambridge, UK: Pisces Publications and BirdLife International.
